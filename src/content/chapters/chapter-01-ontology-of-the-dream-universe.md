What Exists When the World Is Asleep 

Dream physics begins with a radical premise: 

Dreams are not illusions. 
They are realities organized by different laws. 

Where waking physics is governed by mass, energy, linear time, and external causation, the 
Dream Universe is governed by association, emotion, memory, and symbolic resonance. Matter 
there is not made of particles but of meaning-structures, which behave as dynamically as atoms 
but obey wholly different rules. 

To study the dream universe scientifically requires an ontology: 

a catalog of what actually exists in dream-space — its fundamental “stuff.” 

1. The Fundamental Substance: Oneiros 

The base unit of dream reality is not energy, nor matter, nor light. 

It is: 

Oneiros (Ονειρος) — Perceptual Matter 

Definition: 

Oneiros is the raw experiential substance of dreams — the perceptible “stuff” 
from which images, sensations, emotions, identities, and narratives are 
formed. 

Oneiros behaves like a hybrid between: 

-  sensory perception

-  emotional feeling

-  narrative expectation

It does not occupy space — it defines space. 

Oneiros does not move through time — it becomes time. 

In waking reality, perception is a secondary effect occurring inside matter. 

In dream reality, matter is a secondary effect occurring inside perception. 

All dream objects — landscapes, people, flying houses, screaming clocks, fractal staircases — 
are stabilized crystallizations of Oneiros. 

We call these stable Oneiros concentrations: 

O-Nodes (Oneiric Nodes) 

Definition: 

Any temporarily persistent dream-form whose boundaries appear stable to 
the dreamer. 

Examples: 

-  A dream body

-  A city that lasts several minutes

-  A recurring dream symbol (the hallway, the ocean, the teeth falling out)

O-Nodes are not solid. 

They are more like tight whirlpools of meaning spinning inside perceptual fluid. 

2. The Symbol Field 

Oneiros does not self-organize randomly. 

It condenses within a force-field: 

The Symbol Field 

Definition: 

A global, nonlocal shaping layer where collective myth, personal memory, 
archetype, and cultural imagery merge into probability densities. 

The Symbol Field is composed of: 

-  Archetypes

-  Language fragments

-  Cultural symbols

-  Personal memory echoes

-  Emotional imprints

Dream imagery is pulled from this field via Symbolic Resonance: 

The dream-mind attracts symbols that vibrationally match the emotional state 
being experienced. 

Sadness attracts abandonment imagery. 

Desire attracts reunion imagery. 

Fear attracts chase imagery. 

The dream doesn’t decide “what to show.” 

The Symbol Field automatically supplies matching images. 

Symbol density varies between dreamers: 

• Artists → thick, surreal Symbol Field generation 

• Trauma survivors → high recurrence loops 

• Psychedelic explorers → expanded field bandwidth 

• Lucid dreamers → partial symbol steering abilities 

3. Emotional Fields 

Dream reality is shaped by emotion the way spacetime is curved by mass. 

Affective Fields 

Definition: 

Gradient zones of dream reality formed by the intensity and coherence of 
emotion. 

Every emotion functions as a localized gravity well: 

-  Love bends scenes toward union

-  Terror stretches landscapes into infinite pursuit

-  Confusion fragments space into disconnected rooms

-  Ecstasy melts boundaries entirely

This yields the First Dream Equation: 

Scene Stability ∝ Emotional Coherence / Memory Fragmentation 

Interpretation: 

Dream scenes remain stable only when emotional currents are clear and focused. 

When emotion scatters or memory fractures, environments warp, melt, or jump. 

Dream instability is not error. 

It is physics. 

4. Mnemonic Mass (Memory Gravity) 

Memory behaves as mass does: 

Mnemonic Mass 

Definition: 

The gravitational pull exerted by emotionally-charged memories upon dream 
formation. 

Large memories distort dream-realities toward familiar narratives: 

-  Childhood homes recur endlessly

-  Former partners appear with near-inevitability

-  Traumatic sites re-manifest as endless labyrinths

The stronger the unresolved emotion attached to a memory, the more “massive” it becomes — 
warping perception toward repetition loops. 

This produces: 

Memory Wells 

When dreams orbit fixed storylines: 

-  Always running from something

-  Always looking for someone

-  Always late for something

These loops represent stable gravitational basins of unresolved meaning rather than narrative 
flaws. 

5. Self-Topology: Identity Superposition 

In waking physics, one object occupies one position at a time. 

Dream identity does not obey this restriction. 

Identity Superposition 

Definition: 

The dream-self exists simultaneously as observer, participant, narrator, and 
sometimes multiple characters. 

You may be: 

• Yourself AND your childhood self 

• Yourself AND the aggressor 

• Yourself AND the landscape 

Dream consciousness is therefore multi-positional — a form of experiential quantum state. 

We define the dream “self” not as a single body, but as a nexus of perspectives: 

The I-Nexus 

The I-Nexus is the experiential center tying multiple simultaneous identity states into a unified 
awareness. 

Lucidity modifies the I-Nexus by allowing partial selection of identity placements. 

6. Dream Space Geometry 

Dream environments appear spatial but do not obey Euclidean geometry. 

They follow: 

Symbolic Topology 

Rules include: 

-  Distance equals emotional narrative separation

-  Doors connect places sharing symbolic affinity, not physical proximity

-  Scale responds to emotional salience:

  -  What matters grows larger

  -  What is ignored shrinks or dissolves

Dream-space is therefore defined as: 

A topology of meaning, not a geometry of meters. 

You may walk through a hallway and emerge into: 

• Childhood school classroom 

• Familiar grocery store 

• A surreal void with floating kittens 

These are not breaches of logic — they are lawful symbolic adjacency transitions. 

7. Time in the Dream Universe 

Dream time does not flow. 

Kairotemporal Physics 

Definition: 

Dream time progresses according to psychological relevance rather than 
duration. 

Thus: 

-  A dream may contain decades of narrative within seconds

-  Scenes advance only when emotional resolution changes state

-  Time loops until symbolic closure occurs

Time is not measured in seconds. 

Time is measured in: 

Emotional Phase Shifts 

Events occur when emotion changes. 

Not before. 

Not after. 

Only during shifts of feeling. 

8. Reality Status of Dreams 

We now arrive at a philosophical cornerstone: 

Dreams are not fake. They are differently-configured perceptual universes. 

They do not occupy shared physical coordinates, but they occupy valid experiential coordinates. 

Dream worlds are: 

• Personally instantiated 

• Symbolically real 

• Perceptually coherent 

• Governed by consistent laws 

They are therefore ontologically legitimate realities despite lacking shared macroscopic 
persistence.

10. Field Exercises – Chapter 1 

Exercise A – Symbol Mapping 

Before sleep, identify: 

• 1 unresolved memory 

• 1 dominant emotional state 

Observe whether these appear symbolically in dreams. 

Document emotional associations rather than narrative consistency. 

Exercise B – Emotional Gravity Tracking 

On waking from a dream: 

Ask: 

-  What emotion governed the dream?

-  Where did scenery distort most strongly?

Map distortions to emotional spikes. 

Exercise C – Identity Superposition Audit 

Track moments where you witnessed yourself from outside or inhabited multiple perspectives. 

This maps fluctuations in the I-Nexus configuration. 

Chapter 1 Conclusion 

We now possess a rigorous ontology of dream-space: 

Dream reality is built of perception matter (Oneiros), sculpted by emotional gravity fields and 
symbolic probability currents, anchored by memory mass and navigated by a superpositional 
multi-self known as the I-Nexus. 

Space is not distance. 

Time is not duration. 

Identity is not singular. 

Dreams operate as a fully coherent reality whose physics is emotional, symbolic, and mnemonic 
rather than material.
